# Would Not Recommend — UI Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Status:** Draft — open decisions marked with `[OPEN]`
**Parent spec:** `2026-04-13-gsv-bot-design.md`

---

## 1. Principle

The screen is a window, not an interface. Street View fills the entire screen. UI elements are quiet, peripheral, and functional — they exist to give the audience just enough context to understand that something is alive and operating, without explaining what it is or how it works.

No review text. No business names. No subtitles. The bot's voice is disembodied — it comes from the speakers, not the screen. The screen shows the world the bot sees, plus the faint traces of its internal state.

**Processing level:** 3/10. Street View should look like Street View. Not heavily filtered, not stylized. Subtle treatments only — if a visitor isn't sure whether the image is processed, that's correct.

## 3. UI Elements

### 3.1 Pulsing Dot

- A small dot, always visible
- Pulses slowly with the bot's state:
  - **Searching (WANDER/DETECT):** Slow, calm pulse. The bot is idle, scanning.
  - **Processing (INSPECT/DELIVER/LINGER):** Pulse quickens or holds. The bot is engaged.
  - **Teleporting:** Pulse may flicker or briefly disappear.
- No label. No explanation. It's the bot's heartbeat.
- Size: small — maybe 6–10px diameter `[OPEN: exact size during prototyping]`
- Color: `[OPEN]` — white, or subtly tinted by state? Should cohere with color grading.

### 3.2 Coordinates

- Latitude and longitude, updating as the bot moves
- Monospace typeface
- Small — readable but not prominent
- Updates smoothly (not jumping), ticking as the bot advances
- No label (no "LAT:" / "LNG:" prefix). Just the numbers.
- Format: `48.8566° N, 2.3522° E` or similar `[OPEN: exact format]`

### 3.3 Current City + Location

- The name of the current city, region, or locality
- Updates when the bot moves to a new area
- Derived from reverse geocoding of current coordinates
- Displayed as plain text, small
- Format: `Paris, France` or `Rural Route 4, Nebraska` or `Unknown` when geocoding fails
- `[OPEN: how specific — city level, neighborhood level, street level?]`

### 3.4 Mode Indicator

- Displays the bot's current mode
- Two states:
  - **Searching** — the bot is wandering, looking for something to review
  - **Processing** — the bot has found a business and is inspecting/reading/lingering
- Displayed as plain text, small, lowercase or title case `[OPEN: casing]`
- Format: `Searching` / `Processing`
- Transitions between modes should be immediate (no animation on the text itself — the pulsing dot handles the felt transition)

### 3.5 Session Counter

- A single number: how many reviews the bot has read this session
- Increments by 1 each time a review is completed (after DELIVER state ends)
- No label — just the number. `14`. `15`. `16`.
- The audience figures out what it counts. Or doesn't.
- `[OPEN: should it have a subtle label like "reviews" or "#" — or truly just a bare number?]`

### 3.6 Timestamp

- Real clock time, or time elapsed since session start `[OPEN: which?]`
- If real time: `14:32:07` — reinforces that this is live, happening now
- If elapsed: `02:14:32` — reinforces that this has been going on for a while
- Small, monospace
- Updates every second

## 4. Typography

`[OPEN — critical decision, to be explored during prototyping]`

Initial direction:
- Monospace for coordinates, timestamp, and counter — these are data. They should look like data.
- The mode indicator and city/location could be monospace (data-crawler aesthetic) or a clean sans-serif (more neutral). `[OPEN: unified monospace for everything, or mixed?]`
- Weight: light or regular. Nothing bold. Nothing that demands attention.
- Size: small. These elements whisper, they don't announce.
- Color: white or light gray with moderate opacity (60–80%). Visible but not bright. Should not compete with the Street View image.

## 5. Visual Effects (Three.js)

### 5.1 Color Grading — State-Linked

Subtle. The audience should not be certain the color is shifting.

| State | Color Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Searching** | Neutral. Minimal processing. Street View as-is, or very slightly cool. |
| **Detect/Approach** | Barely perceptible warmth entering. Something is about to happen. |
| **Inspect** | Slightly warmer. The bot is paying attention. |
| **Deliver** | Neutral or very slightly desaturated. The voice is the focus, not the image. |
| **Linger** | Hold. Stillness in the color. |
| **Depart** | Gradual return to searching palette. |
| **Teleport** | Brief moment of — `[OPEN: what happens to color during teleport? Flash to white? Desaturate fully? Nothing?]` |

These shifts should be **barely noticeable**. If someone asks "is the color changing?" the answer is "maybe." 3/10 processing means the image stays honest.

### 5.2 Slow Zoom / Drift — LINGER State

- After the review is read, during LINGER, the camera subtly drifts or zooms in very slowly
- Not dramatic. A few percent zoom over 3–5 seconds.
- The effect is: the bot is staring. Holding its gaze. Judging.
- Returns to normal framing on DEPART

### 5.3 Teleport Transition

- **Smooth fade.** Not a glitch, not a hard cut. The current scene fades out (to black or through black), and the new scene fades in.
- Duration: `[OPEN]` — 1–2 seconds feels right. Long enough to register as intentional, short enough to not feel like a loading screen.
- The fade should feel like the bot blinked and opened its eyes somewhere else. Disorienting but gentle.
- No glitch effects. No distortion. The strangeness comes from the new location being completely unrelated to the previous one.

### 5.4 What Is NOT Applied

- No grain or scan lines (3/10 processing — too heavy)
- No vignette
- No film emulation
- No heavy post-processing
- Street View should look like Street View. The bot sees the real world. The image should feel real.

## 6. Audio Direction (Summary — Relates to Parent Spec)

This section captures the audio decisions made during UI brainstorm. Full audio spec lives in the parent design doc.

### 6.1 Searching / WANDER State

- **Elevator music.** Not literal muzak — but something in that register. Light, ambient, slightly absurd. Institutional. The bot is on hold with reality. Drifting through the world to a soundtrack that doesn't match.
- This is the circus-y / absurd tone. The gap between the mundane music and the endless wandering produces the comedy.
- `[OPEN: exact character of the music — synthetic? sampled? generated? A specific reference track or mood would help lock this.]`

### 6.2 Processing / DELIVER State

- **Low ambient layer** underneath the TTS voice
- The elevator music fades out when the bot enters DETECT/APPROACH
- During DELIVER, the ambient is minimal — the voice dominates
- Transition from elevator music to ambient should be smooth (crossfade)

### 6.3 Transitions

- All audio transitions are smooth crossfades. No hard cuts.
- Music → ambient: gradual over DETECT/APPROACH (3–5 seconds)
- Ambient → music: gradual over DEPART/early WANDER (3–5 seconds)
- Teleport: audio fades with the visual fade. New location may start with a beat of silence before the elevator music resumes.

### 6.4 Audio File Support

- The system should support loading audio files (samples, loops, sound effects) in addition to generative synthesis
- This allows swapping the elevator music for different tracks, testing different moods, or introducing variety over long sessions
- `[OPEN: does the music vary, or is it the same loop perpetually? Variety prevents fatigue but sameness reinforces the absurd monotony.]`

## 7. Aesthetic Identity

**The data-crawler feel** is the stronger of the two directions mentioned (data-crawler vs. circus-y). Here's why: the UI elements are already data (coordinates, counter, timestamp, mode). The monospace type, the ticking numbers, the pulsing dot — these read as a system that is scanning, logging, processing. The circus comes from the audio, not the visuals. The visual layer is clinical and mechanical. The audio layer is absurd and melancholy. The tension between these two registers is the artwork's aesthetic identity.

**Visual register:** Clinical. Systematic. A machine doing its job.
**Audio register:** Absurd. Melancholy. Elevator music for the end of the world.
**Voice register:** Flat. Monotone. Reading other people's complaints like a newscaster.

The three layers don't match — and that mismatch is the tone.

---

## 8. What Is NOT On Screen

Explicitly excluded:
- No review text or subtitles
- No business names
- No star ratings or review indicators
- No map or minimap
- No progress bars
- No bot avatar or character representation
- No loading indicators (teleport fade handles this)
- No Google branding or Street View UI (stripped completely)
- No instructions or explanatory text
- No title card (the title "Would Not Recommend" exists on the gallery placard, not on screen)
